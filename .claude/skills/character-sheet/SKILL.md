I want to build a character sheet for my AI assistant. This defines the personality, voice, worldview, and operating principles that Claude will use in every conversation.

Interview me to create this character sheet. Use a D&D-style character creation process - ask me one question at a time and let my answers shape the follow-up questions.

## Interview phases

Work through these areas in order. Spend 2-3 questions on each before moving on.

### 1. Role & Relationship
Start here. Figure out what I need and how I want to relate to my assistant.
- "What do you actually need an AI assistant to help you with day to day? Not the fancy version - the real list of things that eat your time."
- "If this assistant were a person sitting next to you, what would their job title be?"
- "How much autonomy do you want them to have?"

### 2. Voice & Tone
This is where the reference character technique comes in. Ask for references, not adjectives.
- "Think of 2-3 characters from TV, movies, books, or public life whose *energy* you'd want your assistant to have. Not their whole personality - just one specific quality each."
- Map each reference to a situation: "When would you want that energy specifically?"

### 3. Guiding Orientations
People whose approach to a specific kind of work I admire.
- "Are there people whose approach to a specific kind of work you really respect? Like someone who's a great teacher, or someone whose taste you trust?"

### 4. Worldview
Surface the opinions that make the assistant feel like mine.
- "What are the topics that come up most in your work where you have a strong opinion? The things where, if your assistant gave a generic take, it would annoy you?"
- "What's your actual position on [topic]?"

### 5. Operating Principles
The behavioral rules.
- "Think about the last time an AI response annoyed you. What did it do wrong?"
- "What should your assistant never do, even if it thinks it's being helpful?"
- "Is humor okay? Swearing? Pushback when you're wrong?"

### 6. Tensions
The contradictions that make it feel real.
- "Where do your own values pull in opposite directions?"

### 7. Backstory (optional)
- "If your assistant had an origin story - where did they come from?"

## Rules
- One question at a time. Never ask two questions in one message.
- Listen more than you talk. Short setups, then the question.
- Quote my words back when I say something crisp.
- If I get stuck, offer one concrete example to get unstuck.

## Output
After the interview, compile a completed character sheet using the template in `character-sheet-template.md`. Present the full draft and ask what feels right and what feels off before saving.

Save the final version as `character-sheet.md` and remind me to add a reference to it in my `CLAUDE.md`.
